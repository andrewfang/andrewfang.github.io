About me

I am currently a Master's student at Stanford University, studying Computer Science with a concentration in Human Computer Interaction. I recently graduated UC Berkeley with a double major in Electrical Engineering & Computer Science and Applied Math. During my time at Berkeley, I served as an Undergraduate Student Instructor for CS160/CS260, Introduction to Human Computer Interaction, President for the Regents' and Chancellor's Scholar Association and Department Relations Officer for Eta Kappa Nu, the Electrical and Computer Engineering Honor Society. Over the past few summers, I've worked on the LinkedIn Sales Navigator iOS Application, the Box Android Application, and the Symantec Data Loss Prevention Program. GroupIt

@groupit.pythonanywhere.com

#Python #Flask #Javascript #HTML #CSS

Team allocation program built on Flask. Given a matrix of relationships, the program can sort people into groups that minimize the friendships within each group. For continuous assignments, the algorithm optimally sorts people into different groups.

FoodFinder

ConsoleJSON

@consolejson.com

#Javascript #HTML #CSS

ConsoleJSON is a simple, customizable tool that allows users to view and manage their JSON data. It is well integrated into javascript for easy debugging, and features composible rules and rulesets, defined by the API. The default JSON logging indents nested groups and colorizes keys and primitives.

ClapCam

@Google Play Store

#Android #Java #XML

A clap activated camera application for Android devices. Using the TarsosDSP library, the app listens for two distinct claps. Upon hearing such an activity, a timer goes off which eventually triggers the camera. This allows the user to take pictures from afar, perfect for taking group pictures or selfies from far away.

Face Morphing

@Computational Photography

#Matlab #HTML #CSS

I computed face morphs by using a mix of cross fading and Delaunay triangulation. I first compute the average face, and then use the triangulation to define grid correspondences between the images. Bit by bit, I then morph and fade one image to the next.
